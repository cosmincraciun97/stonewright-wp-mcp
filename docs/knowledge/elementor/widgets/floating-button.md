## Purpose
A floating button is a persistent, clickable element that remains visible as users scroll through your website, typically positioned in a corner. It encourages specific actions like contacting support, subscribing, or initiating conversations without requiring visitors to navigate back to a fixed location.

## Use this when
- You want to maintain visibility of a call-to-action throughout the entire page
- Encouraging visitors to take a specific action (contact, subscribe, call)
- Creating a persistent contact or messaging option
- Improving conversion rates by keeping important CTAs accessible
- Building mobile-friendly experiences where screen space is limited

## Settings highlights
- **Button text and styling**: Customize appearance to match your site design
- **Position settings**: Choose fixed corner placement (top/bottom, left/right)
- **Visibility controls**: Set which pages or devices display the button
- **Animation effects**: Add entrance animations or hover effects for engagement
- **Link destination**: Direct clicks to forms, pages, external links, or phone numbers
- **Display conditions**: Show/hide based on scroll position, user behavior, or device type
- **Z-index management**: Control layering to ensure proper display above other content
- **Responsive adjustments**: Different sizing and positioning for desktop vs. mobile

## Limits / gotchas
- Excessive floating elements can clutter the interface and frustrate users
- Mobile positioning requires careful testing to avoid obscuring critical content
- Performance may be impacted with complex animations on lower-end devices
- Display conditions must be thoughtfully configured to avoid annoying visitors unnecessarily
